Understanding Image Compression: Why it Matters for Your Web Workflow

Sarah Jenkins Senior Editor

Image compression is a vital part of the modern web. Without it, pages would load slowly, and user experience would suffer. In this deep dive, we explore the difference between lossy and lossless compression and how ImageHub Pro can help you optimize your workflow.

Lossy vs. Lossless

When you save an image, you often have to choose between quality and file size. Lossy compression removes some data from your image to reduce the file size significantly. This is great for web use where speed is key.

On the other hand, lossless compression keeps all the data intact. This is crucial for archiving or when you need to edit the image later without compounding artifacts.

"Optimizing your images is the single most effective way to speed up your website loading time without touching code."
